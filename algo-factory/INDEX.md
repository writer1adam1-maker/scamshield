## The 4 Algorithms (Physics & Game Theory Perspective)

### 1. Intent Field Analyzer
**Type:** Physics-based (field theory, thermodynamics)
**Problem:** Detect novel phishing forms without obfuscation signatures
**Solution:** Model malicious intent as conserved energy field flowing through form topology
**Speed:** 45ms-1.2s | **Accuracy:** Catches novel variants

### 2. Adversarial Payoff Reconstruction
**Type:** Game-theoretic (Nash equilibrium, mechanism design)
**Problem:** Identify zero-day attacks before they're in databases
**Solution:** Invert attacker optimization; detect non-Nash forms as anomalies
**Speed:** 50-2000ms | **Accuracy:** Strategic deviation detection

### 3. Behavioral Phase Transition Tracker
**Type:** Physics-based (critical phenomena, dynamical systems)
**Problem:** Detect coordinated attacker evolution and shared intelligence
**Solution:** Track population-level phase transitions revealing ecosystem-wide changes
**Speed:** 50-100ms | **Accuracy:** Population-level patterns

### 4. Deception Cascade Fragility Index
**Type:** Structural analysis (systems theory, dependency graphs)
**Problem:** Optimize defenses against cascading deception tricks
**Solution:** Measure trick dependencies and critical failure points
**Speed:** 100-4000ms | **Accuracy:** Identifies weak links

## Core Innovations

### Physics Perspective
- **Treat malicious intent as conserved quantity** flowing through form topology
- **Detect phase transitions** in attack population diversity
- **Use thermodynamic relaxation** to find equilibrium form designs
- **Measure critical phenomena** to predict system sensitivity

### Game Theory Perspective
- **Invert attacker optimization** to detect novel strategies
- **Solve Nash equilibria** to understand attack-defense coevolution
- **Model mechanism design** to optimize our defenses
- **Track evolutionary stable strategies** to predict attacker evolution

### Why This Matters
These aren't ML classifiers or signature patterns. They're **structural approaches** that detect attacks by understanding what attacks *must* do to function. No obfuscation or polymorphism can escape:
- Conserved intent flow (you must exfiltrate data somewhere)
- Rational optimization deviations (new strategies deviate from equilibrium)
- Population coordination (sharing intelligence leaves phase transition signatures)
- Cascading tricks (every deception has critical failure points)
